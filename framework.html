The Framework

The CPA framework.

A modular, accredited body of knowledge. Authored from African enterprise reality. In delivery on three continents.

i.

Modular by design

A composable IP library. Modules are assembled per function and per market, not pulled from a fixed shelf. Cohorts get the curriculum they actually need.

ii.

In-context, always

The curriculum is taught in the language, the constraints, and the case-studies of the function being trained. HR practitioners learn from HR cases. Operations leads learn from operations cases. No retrofits.

iii.

Accredited credentials

CPA programmes carry recognised CPD hours and are gated behind certified senior practitioners and AI-supported aftercare. The credential means something at the end of the cohort, and three years later.

iv.

Continuous practice

Annual top-ups maintain practitioner status. Capability is treated as a perishable asset, not a graduation event.

Where it came from

Authored on the continent. Held in trust.

Most agile bodies of knowledge were written elsewhere and exported here. CPA was written here, refined in the demanding day-to-day of African banks, insurers and operations companies, and only then offered to global delivery.

The economic and cultural reality of African enterprise is not an afterthought to CPA. It is the design brief. The framework holds because it was tested against the real conditions of professional work in markets that do not forgive imported assumptions.

"We did not localise an existing standard. We authored a standard, locally, and exported it."
Founding Principle . Professional Agile
